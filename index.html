Abstract

Despite significant advances in speech processing technologies, a substantial number of the world’s languages and their dialects remain under-resourced. This disparity contributes to a widening technological gap, thereby impeding broader technological inclusion. One obstacle to inclusion is the scarcity of comprehensive datasets for developing robust multidialectal Text-to-Speech (TTS) systems. In this work, we present SIRAJ, an extensive dataset of Arabic dialects for dialectal Text-to-Speech systems. SIRAJ contains audio, transcription, speaker gender, and dialect information for six Arabic dialects: Egyptian, Emirati, Jordanian, Saudi, Moroccan, and Syrian. We find that fine-tuning on SIRAJ affords human-like audio quality in generated samples for some dialects. By establishing strong baselines, we hope that SIRAJ can serve as a basis for future work in TTS for Arabic dialects.
